The Open Marketplace

Any writer, psychologist, urban planner, or teenager can author a dictionary and publish it on the Sage Registry. Authors keep 92% of every sale. The platform takes 8% — what infrastructure actually costs with a fair margin. Dictionaries can be sold, gifted, time-loaned, or open-sourced.

§05 — The Private Domain

The mind cannot
be a prison.

Encrypted on-device. No server holds it. No policy can surface it. Any thought, feeling, or desire — logged without judgment, never exposed.

Silent Connections Protocol

Two users independently log mutual affection. Both have signed the non-harassment contract. The system runs a cryptographic match locally — revealing only the binary result to both simultaneously. Content never exposed to anyone, including the platform.

Minor Protection Framework

Date of birth required at registration. State-compiled legal parameters determine node access for minors. Parents set permissions — not content visibility. Individual rights are the philosophy; legal compliance is the floor.

§07 — The Mathematical Moat

tags × questions = 1 result

No list. Ever.

10 parameter tags. 10 sequential clarifying questions. Each bisects the candidate space. 10,000 candidates becomes 1. Hard constraints are never silently violated — the engine surfaces conflicts and asks.

Enterprises must speak STARDUST to appear in results. Compliance is not gatekeeping — it is the technical precondition for the promise to hold. Switching costs compound with every integration.
